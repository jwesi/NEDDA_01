COULD I GET A LIGHT?

Cigarettes weren’t always a crutch for me. I began smoking while at SAIC. Every night the students in 162, a dorm for SAIC, would go to a nearby alley to smoke their various substances. Groups of people who wouldn’t have met otherwise would mingle. You would see people you wanted to talk to all week. Or someone completely new. There was a strong community in the alley. After leaving Chicago, I found myself missing the community I found in the alley. To stay connected to them I would continue the habit. Which turned into a dependency. I found myself using cigarettes as a cure-all when it came to any number of things that could be plaguing me at a time. Perhaps most importantly, cigarettes became my favorite

accessory.

10 green lights home

On Cloudy days I would look up to the sky towards the horizon. I would stand outside perfectly still. I would wait and watch the direction the clouds were moving in. I would search for the golden light that meant the sun was breaking through. If I couldn’t see It I would feel this churning in my stomach. This slight fear of what may be coming. The uncertanty in the darkness on the horizon would worry me. I’d continue to wait inside, staring through every window in any direction they would face. Searching the horizon. I would search until I finally saw that golden line on the edge of the clouds. I find myself doing the same thing now. Invisioning my life on a graph all around me. The future shrouded in darkness full of possibilies with countless affects. I stay perfectly still, searching for that golden light on the horizon.

I think I’m going to die in obsurity.
