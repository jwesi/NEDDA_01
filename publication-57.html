I have a really hard time explaining myself. I don’t know if I learned to completely allow myself to think through and verbalize my thoughts and actions. Only giving someone the clip notes as the reasoning behind my behaviors. Allowing my “I don’t know”s and inaudible mumblings fill the silence that should be a response. From the time you ask me “Why did you do this?” Or “Why do you think that?” I instantly second guess myself. As if your questioning of my behavior or thinking has invalidated what I’ve done. Instead of contesting this or allowing you to understand me, I hide my motives away from everyone. Most frustratingly, I even hide them from myself. I do have a certain fear of criticism. Only because I see someone criticizing me as a personal failure. As though I’ve allowed someone to get too close to me. I’ve allowed someone to see my flaws. How could I be loved and flawed? I do not think I have the luxury to be both. I do not think I have the luxury to tell you why I’ve done something without contention. I do not believe that I have that luxury, to be loved after the contention.

This plays the a large part in conversations about my future. Especially when I’m asked if I want to be an artist. I find this to be one of the most offensive questions to be asked. It never feels innocent. Art is not something that I fell into nor something that I continue to pursue out of force. It is not even something I simply would like to do with my life. I cannot conceive or fathom a future with me being completely removed from art as a career. This question is comparable to someone asking me if I am good enough to pursue art. The answer to that being I don’t know if I am good enough. It’s all I want to do. It’s all I can see myself doing. But am I good enough? Can I hold my work up proudly to the level of those who inspired me to pursue this? I have no idea. Yet these quandaries are not enough to stop me from continuing my practice. Not at the moment. I pray that moment never comes.

to create something beautiful

beyond the time and space you inhabit

beyond the people you showed

I hope to have that gift.
