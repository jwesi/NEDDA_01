Pokemon Legends Arceus became one of my favorite games of 2022. With my apphorant mental health and sleep schedule, it was nice to get lost in the games that comforted me as a child. I couldn’t put it down days during winter interim. It dealt with themes surprisingly close to what I had been grappling with. Themes like god, the origin of the universe, and your place in it. I was astonished how deep the story felt at the time. Looking back it seems a bit silly. However, In the dead of winter I could explore a new world that changed the series. It allowed me to stop staring at the walls in my room getting lost in the horrors my brain would conjure and into a bright and colorful place. The cut scenes were a bit long (no voice acting all reading) I was still left in awe by the time I finished it. I still go back to it. Yet even with the, albeit short, time that has passed. I still have that feeling that it gave me before. When I felt completely alone, this game arguably made for children comforted me. There were very few things that comforted me. I couldn’t find many things to entertain myself with. Especially not in ways that were substantial enough for me to stop watching Golden Girls on repeat. This game helped me through the cold days and starless nights. I really appreciate Pokemon Legends Arceus, even with all the flaws I would encounter during each play through. It came at the perfect time for me. Revived my childhood and brought some light to an extremely dark time. For that, I will always be grateful.
